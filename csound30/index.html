25th - 27th November 2016

Three days of music and discussion to mark 30 years of Csounding.





Keynote Seminar:

Tarmo Johannes:

The Computer Duo - technology as both a tool and a partner in performance.

Tarmo Johannes is a versatile musician currently playing flute in a variety of contemporary music ensembles. Since 2009 he has been actively involved using Csound that has turned into an essential part of his creative output - interactive sound-games, computer aided performance tools, performances of electro- acoustic music, sound installations. Tarmo Johannes is currently also the main developer of CsoundQt, the most used Csound front-end.

Call for Submissions:

Submissions of pieces, papers and workshops for this conference are welcomed. Unfortunately we will be unable to host sound installations. The deadline for abstracts, music and workshop proposals is Friday 14th October at Midnight. Notification of acceptance will be given before Wednesday 19st October.
Paper presentations should last for no longer than 20 minutes.
Music submissions should be of a maximum duration of 12 minutes and should be accompanied by a description of any technical requirements.
Workshops should be designed to last for maximum 1 hour.
All submissions should be accompanied by a short biography.
Please also indicate whether you will be attenting the conference. (Paper presentations cannot be given via Skype etc.) In music selection, preference will be given to composers who will be attending.

Submissions can be made to:
Iain.McCurdy "at" nuim "dot" ie or any of the other conference organisers named below.


Registration:

Registration is free but if you will be attending, please contact one of the conference organisers to allow us to better plan activities. Please also inform us of any organisation affiliation you have, what country you are travelling from and your dates of arrival and departure.

In the meantime please feel free to write to any of us informally or contact us through the Csound mailing list with questions or suggestions.

We look forward to seeing you in November!

Brian Carty, Iain McCurdy, Rory Walsh and Victor Lazzarini

Strategieën en tips


From the airport to Maynooth by bus/train:

A direct bus service is provided by Dualway Airport Hopper. €7.20 single. Journey time is approx 45 minutes.

If you prefer to travel via Dublin city centre take the Dublin Bus 747 (25 mins/€6.00 single) as far as Connolly train station and from there, take a train direct to Maynooth (40 mins/€5.50). Hint: trains to Sligo also stop at Maynooth.

Campus Map:


The Bewerunge Room, O'Callaghan Room and the New Music Room are all in the music department in Logic house on the south campus (7 on the map).
Riverstown Hall is also on the south campus (21 on the map).
The students union is on the top edge of the north campus.
